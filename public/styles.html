The rules of APA 7th, encoded.

Author shape, title sentence case, italics, DOI prefix, and the article–number rule for online journals — every line below is implemented in src/shared/citation/styles/apa7.ts.

01

Author format

Family, F. M. — comma-then-initials. Use ampersand before the final author. List up to 20 authors; for 21+, list 19, ellipsis, last.

Smith, J. A., Nguyen, T., & Patel, R. K. (2024).
02

Title case

Reference list titles use sentence case — capitalize first word, first word after colon, and proper nouns only. Italicise the journal/site name, not the article title.

On methods of citation. Journal of Library Studies
03

DOI prefix

DOIs are normalised to https://doi.org/… and never end in a full stop. doi:… form is rewritten by APA metascan.

https://doi.org/10.1234/jls.2024.0045
04

Article number rule

Online journals without page ranges may use an article number. APA emits "Article 12" in place of pages.

PLOS ONE, 19(4), Article 12.
05

No-author shape

When no author is given, the title moves into the author position; in-text uses a short title in quotes.

On methods of citation. (2024). Nature. https://www.nature.com/…

The rules of RMIT Harvard, encoded.

Author surname plus initials with no comma; curly single quotes around article titles; doi:… prefix; "accessed" date in Day Month Year format.

01

Author format

Family I — surname then initials, no comma, no period. Use "&" or "and" depending on context.

Smith JA & Nguyen T 2024
02

Curly quotes

Article and chapter titles sit inside curly single quotation marks (U+2018 / U+2019). Webpage and report titles are italicised instead.

'On methods of citation'
03

doi: prefix

Harvard prefers the bare doi:… form (no full stop after).

doi:10.1234/jls.2024.0045
04

Accessed date

For online sources without a clear publication date, append accessed Day Month Year.

accessed 12 March 2024
05

No invented n.p.

When publication place is missing, Harvard does NOT invent "n.p." — leave it out and let the metascan flag it.

Routledge, London.

The rules of IEEE, encoded.

Numbered references in brackets, initials-first author format, and a placeholder [1] users renumber by first appearance in their final document.

01

Bracketed numbers

Each in-text citation is a bracketed number: [1], [2], [3]… Renumber by first appearance when finalising your reference list.

As shown in [1].
02

Initials-first authors

IEEE writes the initials before the surname: J. A. Smith. Use "and" before the last author; up to 6 authors then et al.

J. A. Smith, T. Nguyen, and R. K. Patel
03

Quoted article titles

Article titles are placed in straight double quotes; journal/conference names italicised.

"On methods of citation," Journal of Library Studies
04

DOI form

IEEE accepts doi: 10.… or the full https form. We default to the latter.

doi: 10.1234/jls.2024.0045
05

Renumbering note

Outlined emits [1] as a placeholder. The metascan adds a note reminding you to renumber by final order of appearance.

Note: renumber references by first appearance.
