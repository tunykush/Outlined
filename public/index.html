    How a URL becomes a citation

    01

    Read the page

    Backend safely fetches the HTML — with SSRF protection, 8s timeout, 5MB cap.

    02

    Parse metadata

    JSON-LD, citation_* tags, Open Graph, Dublin Core, Twitter Cards — in priority order.

    03

    Guess the source

    Journal? News article? Blog? The system picks a default, you confirm.

    04

    Apply the rules

    RMIT Easy Cite rules — encoded line by line — produce the formatted reference.

    Faithful to the source

    Encoded directly from RMIT Easy Cite.

    The APA 7th rules in this generator are transcribed page-by-page from RMIT Library's official Easy Cite guide — author lists for 1, 2, 3-20, and 21+ authors; DOI normalisation; the article-number rule for online journals without page ranges; the omission of publisher when it equals the author; hanging-indent reminders. When the rules are open to interpretation, we follow RMIT's published examples.
