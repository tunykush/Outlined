    How a URL becomes a citation

    01

    Read the page

    Backend safely fetches the HTML — with SSRF protection, 8s timeout, 5MB cap.

    02

    Parse metadata

    JSON-LD, citation_* tags, Open Graph, Dublin Core, Twitter Cards — in priority order.

    03

    Guess the source

    Journal? News article? Blog? The system picks a default, you confirm.

    04

    Apply the rules

    RMIT Easy Cite rules — encoded line by line — produce the formatted reference.

    Faithful to the source

    Encoded directly from RMIT Easy Cite.

    The APA 7th and RMIT Harvard rules in this generator are separated into style modules and based on RMIT Library's uploaded Easy Cite guide — author lists for 1, 2, 3-20, and 21+ authors; DOI normalisation; the article-number rule for online journals without page ranges; the omission of publisher when it equals the author; hanging-indent reminders. When the rules are open to interpretation, we follow RMIT's published examples.
